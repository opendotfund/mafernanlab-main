Ongoing Opportunity

Undergraduate Research Assistant

We welcome enthusiastic undergraduate students to join our lab as research assistants. RAs assist with participant recruitment, data collection, and basic data analysis. This is an excellent opportunity to gain hands-on research experience in cognitive neuroscience.

Responsibilities:
  • Assist with participant recruitment and scheduling
  • Help with experimental setup and data collection
  • Contribute to data entry and preliminary analysis
  • Attend lab meetings and participate in discussions

Lab Environment & Culture

Learn what it's like to work in our lab and the values we uphold in our research community.

Collaborative & Inclusive

We foster a collaborative, supportive research environment where diverse perspectives are valued. Our team includes researchers from various backgrounds, disciplines, and career stages, creating a rich, inclusive scientific community.

Innovation & Excellence

We're committed to conducting rigorous, innovative research that advances the field of cognitive neuroscience. We encourage creative thinking, methodological innovation, and the pursuit of challenging research questions.

Mentorship & Growth

Lab members receive guidance on research skills, scientific communication, grant writing, and career planning tailored to their individual goals.

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about joining our lab and the application process.

The required experience level depends on the position. For undergraduate research assistants, no prior research experience is required, though coursework in psychology, neuroscience, or statistics is beneficial. For graduate students and postdoctoral positions, previous research experience is expected. We value enthusiasm, curiosity, and willingness to learn as much as prior experience.

Yes! We value interdisciplinary perspectives and have lab members from various fields including computer science, engineering, linguistics, and philosophy. If you're interested in cognitive neuroscience research and can demonstrate how your background would contribute to our work, we encourage you to apply.

Graduate students in our lab receive funding through a combination of sources:

  • University fellowships and departmental funding
  • Research assistantships on faculty grants
  • Teaching assistantships
  • External fellowships (we mentor students in applying for NSF, NIH, and other grants)

We work with each student to develop a funding plan that provides stable support throughout their graduate training.

Our lab primarily operates in-person due to the nature of our research, which often involves neuroimaging and behavioral testing with participants. However, we offer flexibility with some remote work for specific tasks like data analysis and writing. During the application process, we can discuss specific arrangements based on your role and the current projects.

To strengthen your application:

  • Clearly articulate why you're interested in our research specifically
  • Highlight relevant skills and experiences, even if from different domains
  • Demonstrate familiarity with our recent publications
  • Be specific about which research areas or methods interest you most
  • Reach out to current lab members to learn more about the lab environment
